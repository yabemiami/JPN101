Japanese Adjectives: い & な Adjectives

Grammar: 2 Types of Adjectives
Japanese adjectives are divided into two types based on their conjugations: い & な Adjectives except irregular いい. Adjectives show their earmark when they modify a noun attributively (inside a noun phrase) in a structure:
[NP ADJ NOUN].
Example
  • い: おいしいそば/ delicious soba
    こわい映画えいが/ a scary movie
  • な: げんきな人/ a lively person
    しずかな町まち/ a quiet town
Note:
Grammatically speaking, an attributive adjective modifies a noun inside of a noun phrase as the examples above. On the other hand, there is an predicate adjective. It is an adjective that functions as a predicate of a sentence. In Japanese, the い & な adjectives conjugate differently.

Adjectives 2: い & な Adjectives and conjugations

Grammatical Construction:
When they are used as a predicate of a sentence, the い & な adjectives conjugate differently:
いAffirmativeNegative
Presentさむいですさむくないです
さむくありません
Pastさむかったですさむくなかったです
さむくありませんでした
なAffirmativeNegative
Presentげんきですげんきじゃないです
げんきじゃありません
Pastげんきでしたげんきじゃなかったです
げんきじゃありませんでした
じゃ can be substituted byではwhich sounds more formal; it is used often in the written language
Irr. いいAffirmativeNegative
Presentいいですよくないです
よくありません
Pastよかったですよくなかったです
よくありませんでした
いい can be substituted by よい. It is a relic from Classical Japanese. It is not often used.

Degree Adverbs with Adjectives

Degree Adverbs with Adjectives: すごく , とても, ちょっと
Degree adverbs that can modify the degree of adjectival expressions are placed right before adjectives:
Example
  1. ハワイの海うみは すごく きれいです。/ The sea is very beautiful in Hawaii. {
  2. 台湾たいわんのたべものは とても おいしかったです。/The food in Taiwan was very delicious.}
  3. この部屋へやは ちょっと 寒さむいです/ This room is a little cold.
Note:
You may hear Japanese speakers pronouncing as すごい or すげえ instead すごく. We will stick with すごく.

Like & Dislike with すき(な) & きらい(な)

すき(な) & きらい(な)
Like and Dislike expressions in Japanese are formed with the すき(な)/きらい(な) adjectives applied to what is liked/disliked:
  1. [X-は Yが すきです/きらいです] X like Y/(As for X, Y is favored)
  2. [X-は Yが きらいです] X dislike Y/(As for X, Y is unappealing)
Example
  1. 私わたしは お茶ちゃが すきです/I like tea.
  2. 私わたしは ソーダが きらいです/I dislike soda.
  3. ボブは コーヒーが すきです/Bob likes coffee.
  4. ボブは すしが きらいです/Bob dislikes Sushi.
Note:
You can add だい in front of すき/きらい to emphasize the expressions:
  1. 私わたしは お茶ちゃが 大だいすきです/I like(love) tea very much.
  2. 私わたしは ソーダが 大だいきらいです/I dislike(hate) soda very much.

Let us/Shall we: 〜ましょう/〜ましょうか

Grammatical Construction:
Volitional ましょう(か) expressions are built upon the VerbSTEM-ます form: VerbSTEM-ます ⇒ VerbSTEM-ましょう(か)
Example
  1. 一緒いっしょに ゲームを しましょうか。/Shall we play games together.
  2. スターバックスに 行いきましょう。/Let's go to Starbucks.
  3. どこに 行いきましょうか。/Where shall we go to.
  4. 何なにを しましょうか。/What shall we do.

Counters

Grammatical Construction:
Nouns in Japanese must have a counter expression to be modified by a number just as mass nouns in English (e.g., 2 glasses of water or 4 sheets of paper): [\#+Counter]. There are specific counters for nouns of different natures. We will lean a counter, \#-まい, which is a counter for flat object like sheets of paper, CDs, pancakes, etc. Counters are added immediately in front of or after its modified noun:
  • {[SENTENCE ... (\#+Counter) Noun-(Prt.) (\#+Counter) ...]}
  • [(いちまい) パンケーキを (いちまい) 食たべました]
Example
  1. CDシーディーを　さんまい 買かいます。/I will but three CD.
  2. カードを にまい おくりました (おくる.[u])。 /I sent out two cards.
  3. よんまい パジャンを 食たべました。I ate four Korean Scallion Pancakes.
Note:

Some Adjectives

Grammatical Construction:
Be aware that a set of interpretations conveyed by a word may not be carried over to a equivalent word from another language:
Example
  1. Tom is busy. - トムは 忙いそがしいです。
  2. Tokyo is busy (lively). - 東京とうきょうはにぎやかです。
  3. Tom is lively. -トムは元気げんきです。
  4. 日曜日にちようびは忙いそがしいです。→ (私わたしは) 日曜日にちようびは忙いそがしいです
