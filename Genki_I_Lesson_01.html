The Writing System: げ P20–27

かな
The Japanese writing system uses a combination of Kana and Kanji.
Kana / かな
There are 2 types of syllabary set, called かな:
  • ひらがな hiragana
  • カタカナ katakana
Syllabic structures: V, CV or /n/ (no CVC like English book). Katakana is used for foreign-origin words and some onomatopoeia.

Less. 1.1: [X-は Y です] : げ P41

Grammatical Construction
X-はwa Yですdesu。 = "X is Y."
Example
  1. わたしwatashiはwa がくせいgakuseeですdesu。 = I am a student.
  2. これkoreはwa コンピューターkonpyuutaaです。 = This is a computer.
  3. たなかさんtanaka-sanは にほんじんnihonjinです。 = Mr. Tanaka is Japanese.
Note
  • 〜は is a topic marker: "As for X, it is such that..."
  • Japanese has no conjugation for person/number (cf. English am, are, is).
  • The subject is often dropped: わたしは がくせいです。 = "I am a student."
  • Japanese is verb-final (SOV) — unlike English (SVO) I eat meat, or Chinese (SVO) 我吃肉.
Your own sentence: introduce yourself using Xは Yです.

Less. 1.2: Interrogative particle 〜か : げ P42

Grammatical Construction
Sentence-か。 The suffix 〜か turns a sentence into a question.
Example
  1. これは コンピューターですかka。 = Is this a computer?
  2. たなかさんは にほんじんですか。 = Is Mr. Tanaka Japanese?
  3. これは なんnanですか。 = What is this?
  4. たなかさんは なにじんnani-jinですか。 = Where is Mr. Tanaka from? (person of which nation?)
Note
〜か is used for both yes/no questions (1 & 2) and wh- questions (3 & 4).

Less. 1.3: [Noun1-の-Noun2] : げ P43

Grammatical Construction
In [Noun1-のno-Noun2], Noun1 modifies Noun2.
Example
  1. わたしwatashiのなまえnamae = my name
  2. やまださんyamada-sanのせんこうsenkoo = Mr. Yamada's major
  3. ともだちtomodachiのジョン = (my) friend John
Note
The modification の establishes between two nouns is not just possession (cf. Ex. 3 — ジョン is identified, not possessed).

Kana Usages: Voiced & Double Consonants

Voiced consonants X゛: /k/, /s/, /t/ → /g/, /z/, /d/
  • かきくけこ ⇒ がぎぐげご
  • さしすせそ ⇒ ざじずぜぞ
  • たちつてと ⇒ だぢづでど
/h/ /b/ /p/: は, ば and ぱ
はひふへほ ⇒ ばびぶべぼ ⇒ ぱぴぷぺぽ
Glottalization / double consonant: little っ
A consonant — usually /k/, /s/, /t/, or /p/ — is glottalized when preceded by little っ/ッ:
  • さっか /sakka/ cf. さか /saka/
  • だっし /dasshi/ cf. だし /dashi/
  • たった /tatta/
  • かっぱ /kappa/

Kana Readings: Long Vowels

Vowel lengthening: /aa/ /ii/ /uu/ /ee/ /ei/ /oo/ /ou/
The following two-vowel segments indicate a long vowel that extends over two slots:
  • /aa/ ⇒ /ā/: おばあさん → obāsan
  • /ii/ ⇒ /ī/: おじいさん → ojīsan
  • /uu/ ⇒ /ū/: すう → sū
  • /ee/ & /ei/ ⇒ /ē/: おねえさん → onēsan; えいが → ēga
  • /oo/ & /ou/ ⇒ /ō/: おおい → ōi; おう → ō
Vowel lengthening with Katakana
With katakana, ー indicates a long vowel (two slots):
  • ア / アー = a / aa
  • キャ / キャー = kya / kyaa
  • バタ / バター = bata / bataa
  • タキ / ターキー = taki / taakii

  6. Watching TV with host family at bedtime.
  7. Leaving home.
